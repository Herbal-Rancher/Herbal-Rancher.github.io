## In the Beginning — Slowing Down to See

I’m currently in the **observation phase** of my inductive Bible study using a Precept-style approach. I’ve spent several weeks in Genesis 1, and instead of rushing forward, I’m choosing to slow down even more.

Before moving into deeper interpretation and application, I’m planning to **listen to Genesis twice in full**, then return again to **Genesis 1:2**. My goal is to step back, gain a broader view, and then come back with sharper awareness.
<!--more-->
Even though it feels slow, I’m beginning to see patterns I would have missed otherwise.

---

## What I’m Beginning to See

In just observation alone, I’m noticing how God reveals Himself through repeated actions and roles:

* **God the Father** — Creator, Maker
* **The Holy Spirit** — Moving, hovering, present over the deep
* **The Word** — God *speaking*, calling, bringing things into existence

There is a rhythm forming:

* God **speaks**
* Creation **responds**
* God **sees**
* God **calls/names**
* God **blesses**

This pattern is steady, intentional, and powerful.

---

## Personal Reflection (Observation Only)

One thing that is standing out to me:

> When I feel like I’m in darkness, in deep or formless places,
> the Holy Spirit is not absent — He is already there,
> **hovering, waiting for God to speak.**

This connects deeply with:

> *“In the beginning was the Word…”* (John 1:1)

God speaks — and things change.

---

## Current Focus

Right now, I’m not trying to interpret everything.

I’m focusing on:

* Repetition
* Structure
* Key actions (said, saw, called, blessed)
* Order of creation
* Relationships between what is spoken and what happens
